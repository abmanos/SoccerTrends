This is an interesting visualization of the general trend in price of transfer values in soccer, as it even shows the dip following 2020 from the COVID-19 pandemic. However, there is quite a bit of nuance to this graph, such as large amounts of free transfers (counted as 0) weighing down the transfer value averages and even data points that are set in the future as they've been agreed in advance (small amounts of 2025 and 2026 data). Instead of awkwardly working around that data, we will instead develop a test to see if transfer values have changed significantly over spans of 10 years. As such, we will be looking at two groups, 2003-2013, and 2014-2024, and performing a T-Test to see if there is a significant difference between the mean of the two groups.

For our first statistical method with hypothesis testing, we're going to perform a Two sample T-Test between the two groups to see if there is a statistically significant difference between the means of their transfer fees.

Null Hypothesis: There is not a significant difference between the means of the transfer fees for the 2003-2013 group and the 2014-2024 group

Alternative Hypothesis: There is a significant difference between the means of the transfer fees for the 2003-2013 group and the 2014-2024 group.

Significance level: 5% or 0.05

Moving on to the second statistical method with hypothesis testing, I am going to use a Paired Sample T-Test to check if there is a relation in the means of the transfer fees that were paid for players and their market value.

Null Hypothesis: There is no relation in the means between a player's transfer fee and their market value.

Alternative Hypothesis: There is a relation in the means between a player's transfer fee and their market value.

Significance level: 5% or 0.05

For the third statistical method with hypothesis testing, I am going to run an ANOVA test to see if there is a significant difference in the transfer fees paid by 3 specific teams over the span of this dataset to find out if these 3 teams, who are all in the same league, have had largely the same spending over the time period or if there is a statistically significant difference between any of the groups (teams). The three groups (teams) I will be choosing are Liverpool (the team I support), Manchester United, and Chelsea.

Null Hypothesis: There is no difference in average spending between the 3 groups.

Alternative Hypothesis: There is a difference in the average spending between the 3 groups.

Significance level: 5% or 0.05

If we wanted a more interesting test, we could compare the spending of the 3 groups strictly when they are spending any money.
